Logic Puzzle
The Mystery Switch
Medium
You're in a windowless room with three light switches. Each switch controls a light bulb in another room that you cannot see from where you are. You can flip the switches as many times as you want, but you can only visit the other room ONCE to check the bulbs. How can you determine which switch controls which bulb?
Hint: Think about what properties light bulbs have besides just being on or off...
Click to reveal solution
Solution: The Mystery Switch

Answer:

Turn the first switch on for a few minutes, then turn it off. Turn the second switch on and leave it on. Leave the third switch off the entire time.

Explanation:

When you go into the other room:

  • The bulb that is ON is controlled by the second switch
  • The bulb that is OFF but WARM is controlled by the first switch
  • The bulb that is OFF and COOL is controlled by the third switch

This works because incandescent bulbs generate heat when they're on. By using the heat property, you can determine which switch controlled which bulb even after turning some switches off.

Deductive Reasoning
The Bridge at Night
Medium
Four people need to cross a bridge at night. The bridge is old and can only hold two people at a time. They have one flashlight, and anyone crossing must carry it. The four people walk at different speeds: 1 minute, 2 minutes, 5 minutes, and 10 minutes to cross. What's the fastest time all four can get across?
Hint: Sometimes the fastest person should make multiple trips...
Click to reveal solution
Solution: The Bridge at Night

Answer: 17 minutes

Optimal Strategy:

  1. 1-min and 2-min cross together (2 minutes total)
  2. 1-min returns with flashlight (3 minutes total)
  3. 5-min and 10-min cross together (13 minutes total)
  4. 2-min returns with flashlight (15 minutes total)
  5. 1-min and 2-min cross together (17 minutes total)

Why this works:

The key insight is that the two slowest people (5-min and 10-min) should cross together, not separately. The fastest people shuttle the flashlight back and forth to make this possible efficiently.

The Sequence Mystery
Easy
What comes next in this sequence?

O, T, T, F, F, S, S, E, ?

And what's the pattern?
Hint: Try saying each letter out loud and think about what they might represent...
Click to reveal solution
Solution: The Sequence Mystery

Answer: N (Nine)

Pattern:

Each letter represents the first letter of number names in order:

  • One
  • Two
  • Three
  • Four
  • Five
  • Six
  • Seven
  • Eight
  • Nine

The sequence continues with T (Ten), E (Eleven), T (Twelve), etc.

Spatial Reasoning
The Cube Challenge
Hard
Imagine a 3×3×3 cube made of 27 smaller cubes. If you paint the entire outside surface red, then take the cube apart, how many of the small cubes will have:
  • 3 red faces?
  • 2 red faces?
  • 1 red face?
  • 0 red faces?
Hint: Think about which small cubes are at corners, edges, faces, and inside...
Click to reveal solution
Solution: The Cube Challenge

Answer:

  • 3 red faces: 8 cubes (corner cubes)
  • 2 red faces: 12 cubes (edge cubes, not corners)
  • 1 red face: 6 cubes (face center cubes)
  • 0 red faces: 1 cube (center cube)

Explanation:

3 faces: Corner cubes touch 3 faces (8 corners on a cube)

2 faces: Edge cubes that aren't corners (12 edges × 1 middle cube per edge = 12)

1 face: Center of each face (6 faces × 1 center cube = 6)

0 faces: The single cube in the very center of the 3×3×3

Check: 8 + 12 + 6 + 1 = 27 ✓

Lateral Thinking
The Elevator Problem
Medium
A man lives on the 20th floor of a 30-story building. Every morning he takes the elevator down to the ground floor. When he comes home, he takes the elevator to the 10th floor and walks the rest of the way... except on rainy days, when he takes the elevator all the way to the 20th floor. Why?
Hint: Think about what's different on rainy days that would help him reach higher floors...
Click to reveal solution
Solution: The Elevator Problem

Answer:

The man is too short to reach the button for the 20th floor.

Explanation:

The man can only reach up to the 10th floor button on the elevator panel. However, on rainy days, he carries an umbrella, which he can use to press the 20th floor button.

Alternative solutions that also work:

  • He could ask other passengers to press 20 for him
  • He could use any object he's carrying to reach higher buttons
  • On rainy days, other people might be in the elevator to help

This is a classic lateral thinking puzzle that requires you to think outside conventional assumptions!

The School Subjects
Medium
Three students (Alex, Beth, and Chris) each have a favorite subject (Math, Science, English) and a different grade level (9th, 10th, 11th). Use these clues:
  • Alex is not in 10th grade
  • The student who likes Math is in 11th grade
  • Beth doesn't like English
  • Chris is in 9th grade
  • The 10th grader likes Science
Who likes what subject and what grade are they in?
Hint: Start with what you know for certain and work from there...
Click to reveal solution
Solution: The School Subjects

Answer:

  • Alex: 11th grade, Math
  • Beth: 10th grade, Science
  • Chris: 9th grade, English

Logic:

Chris is 9th grade (given) → Beth must be 10th grade (since Alex isn't) → Alex is 11th grade → Beth likes Science (10th grader) → Alex likes Math (11th grader) → Chris likes English

Hard
Fill in the missing numbers in this 3×3 grid so that every row, column, and diagonal adds up to 15. You can only use the numbers 1-9, and each number can only be used once.

Hint: The center number in a 3×3 magic square is always the target sum divided by 3...

Answer:

How to solve:

Since the center is 5, and we need each row/column/diagonal to sum to 15, the missing bottom center must be 9 (since 4 + ? + 2 = 15, so ? = 9). Wait, that's wrong since 9 is already used!

Word Logic
The Unusual Words
Easy
What do these words have in common?

BANANA, DRESSER, GRAMMAR, POTATO, REVIVE, UNEVEN, ASSESS

Can you think of another word that belongs in this group?
Hint: Try removing one letter at a time from each word and see what happens...
Click to reveal solution
Solution: The Unusual Words

Answer:

If you take away one letter at a time, each word still remains a valid word until you're left with just one letter.

Examples:

  • BANANA → BANAN → BANA → BAN → BA → B
  • DRESSER → DRESSE → DRESS → DRES → DRE → DR → D
  • ASSESS → ASSES → ASSE → ASS → AS → A

Other words that work:

STAIRS, SPOONS, LEARNS, PLATES, etc.

Mathematical Logic
The Age Riddle
Medium
I have two children. The product of their ages is 36, and the sum of their ages is the same as the house number across the street. You look across the street but realize you still need one more piece of information to determine their ages. I then tell you, "My older child loves pizza." Now you know their ages. How old are my children?
Hint: List all the factor pairs of 36 and their sums. Why would you need that extra clue?
Click to reveal solution
Solution: The Age Riddle

Answer: 4 and 9 years old

Step-by-step:

Factor pairs of 36: (1,36), (2,18), (3,12), (4,9), (6,6)

Their sums: 37, 20, 15, 13, 12

The key clue "My older child loves pizza" tells us there IS an older child, so they can't be the same age. This eliminates (6,6).

Since you needed that extra clue, there must have been two pairs with the same sum. Looking at the sums, only 13 appears once, so the house number must be 13.

Therefore: 4 × 9 = 36, and 4 + 9 = 13 ✓

Visual Logic
The Folding Paper
Medium
Take a piece of paper and fold it in half, then in half again, then once more (so 3 folds total). Now punch a hole through all the layers. When you unfold the paper completely, how many holes will there be?

Now predict: if you folded it 4 times and punched one hole, how many holes would appear when unfolded?
Hint: Think about how many layers you're punching through with each fold...
Click to reveal solution
Solution: The Folding Paper

Answer:

3 folds = 8 holes

4 folds = 16 holes

Explanation:

Each fold doubles the number of layers:

  • 0 folds = 1 layer
  • 1 fold = 2 layers
  • 2 folds = 4 layers
  • 3 folds = 8 layers
  • 4 folds = 16 layers

When you punch through all layers and unfold, you get one hole for each layer that was punched.

Formula: Number of holes = 2^(number of folds)

Critical Thinking
The Truth Teller
Hard
You're at a crossroads with two paths. One leads to safety, the other to danger. There are two guards: one always tells the truth, the other always lies, but you don't know which is which. You can ask only ONE question to ONE guard to find the safe path. What question do you ask?
Hint: What would each type of guard say if you asked them what the OTHER guard would tell you?
Click to reveal solution
Solution: The Truth Teller

Answer:

"If I asked the other guard which path leads to safety, what would they say?"

Then take the OPPOSITE path from what they tell you.

Why this works:

  • If you ask the truth-teller: They'll honestly tell you that the liar would point to the dangerous path
  • If you ask the liar: They'll lie about what the truth-teller would say, so they'll also point to the dangerous path

Either way, you'll be directed to the dangerous path, so you choose the opposite!

Pattern Challenge
The Clock Puzzle
Easy
A clock shows 3:00. What's the angle between the hour and minute hands?

Bonus: At what time between 2:00 and 3:00 are the hands exactly on top of each other?
Hint: The hour hand moves continuously, not in jumps. Each hour represents 30 degrees of the circle...
Click to reveal solution
Solution: The Clock Puzzle

Answer:

At 3:00, the angle is 90 degrees

Hands overlap at 2:10:54 (approximately 2:11)

Explanation:

3:00 angle: The minute hand points to 12, the hour hand points to 3. Since each hour mark represents 30° (360° ÷ 12), the angle is 3 × 30° = 90°

Overlap time: The minute hand moves 360° per hour, the hour hand moves 30° per hour. The minute hand gains 330° per hour on the hour hand.

At 2:00, the hour hand is 60° ahead. Time to catch up: 60° ÷ 330° per hour = 2/11 hours = 10.91 minutes ≈ 2:10:54
