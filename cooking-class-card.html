The Haunted Mansion
Easy
Five ghost hunters have just spent time in an allegedly haunted mansion in Whispering Pines, investigating paranormal activity reported by the new owner. Once inside the expansive home, which boasts 20 rooms, the ghost hunters split up and each had a chilling experience! From the clues provided, can you determine the experience each person had and the room they were in at the time?
Logic Grid
Footsteps Shoulder Touch Strange Shadows Eerie Voices Book Fell
Mr Apparition
Shadow Chaser
Specter Sleuth
Spirit Snoopervisor
Hairy Scary
The Haunted Mansion - Clues
1. Shadow Chaser didn't see any spooky shadows during his time in the house. The shadows were not seen in the attic.
2. Neither the ghost hunter who saw a book fall from a shelf nor Mr Apparition had their ghostly experiences in the attic.
3. It was either Spirit Snoopervisor or Hairy Scary who had a memorable experience in the wine cellar - which was filled floor to ceiling with antiques and collectors items, but no wine!
4. While taking some time in the main bedroom to text his wife about dinner, one startled investigator heard slow moving footsteps on the squeaky stairs that he confirmed could not have come from any of the others in the house.
5. Hairy Scary earned her name due to her long, jet-black hair. While she may have looks akin to those of an actress from a Japanese horror film, she was entirely unprepared for the shock of hearing eerie voices on the radio device she was using!

Easy
Pieter's first task of his new job at PR firm "Scandaless" is to organize his supervisor's most commonly used passwords. First he needs to reset them all in case his predecessor is a little bitter (also simply because she came up with terrible passwords), then he needs to record them in a secure location (like a logic puzzle!). From the clues provided, can you determine the old password and the new password for each type of application?

Pity the Passwords - Clues
1. Pieter was a little amused at the use of passwords "password2" and "password3", which were the passwords used for the Client Management and the Social Media applications, but not necessarily in that order.
2. The password "kittycats" was promptly changed to a more secure password, starting with a capital letter V.
3. The two former passwords that were simply the word "password" followed by a number have now been changed to a more secure choice! Neither of them were changed to a password beginning with the lowercase letter "t".
4. As Pieter snickered at his predecessor's use of the password "thisisdumb" for the Document Sharing application, he wondered what he was getting himself into with this new job.
5. The new password for the Video Production application has at least one non-alphanumeric symbol in it. This is also the case for the Social Media application and the Backup Manager.

Highway Murder Mystery - Clues
1. Either the forest ranger or the bloodied shirt were near mile marker 86.
2. The lady walking her dog (a schnauzer cross poodle, otherwise known as a schnoodle) didn't spot any shoe prints.
3. The victim's hair, the item found near mile marker 85, and the clues discovered by the hiker and the scout troop are four different pieces of evidence.
4. The mile marker where the scout troop made camp was one mile more than where the hiker found her piece of evidence.
5. The suspect abandoned his blood stained shirt near mile marker 82.
6. A cigarette butt in the suspect's favorite brand was found at the mile marker one mile less than where the strands of hair were found.
7. The murder weapon was found at mile marker 86. It was purchased earlier that day and still had a price sticker on the handle.
