The Mystery Challenge
Five students are working on different projects for the school science fair. Each student chose a unique topic and is presenting in a different classroom. From the clues provided, can you determine which student is presenting which project and in which room?
Clues to Solve
1. Sarah's project is not about robotics, and she's not presenting in Room 101.
2. The chemistry project is being presented in Room 203, but not by Mike.
3. Alex's presentation room number is higher than Emma's room number.
4. The physics project is being presented by either Sarah or Jake.

Ethical Dilemma

Consider This Scenario:

Your school is implementing a new policy that requires students to turn in their phones at the beginning of each day. The administration says it will improve focus and reduce distractions, but many students argue it violates their privacy and safety.

What's your position? Click to see discussion questions.

Discussion Questions
1. What are the strongest arguments on both sides of this issue?
2. How do you balance individual rights with collective benefits?
3. What alternative solutions might address everyone's concerns?
4. How might this policy affect different groups of students differently?
